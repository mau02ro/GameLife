Game of Life

It is a zero-player game, which means that its evolution is determined by the initial state and does not require any subsequent data entry. The "game board" is a flat mesh made up of squares (the "cells") that extends infinity in all directions. Therefore, each cell has 8 "neighboring" cells, which are next to it, including the diagonal ones. Cells have two states: they are "alive" or "dead" (or "on" and "off"). The state of the cells evolved over discrete units of time (I could say in turns).
